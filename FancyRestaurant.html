Fancy Restaurant Web-app

The Majestic Table Web-app

UX CASE STUDY

Project: To design a menu and payment web-app for a fancy restaurant

Course: Google UX Design Professional (7-month) Certificate course on Coursera.

Project Type: Individual

Duration: 6 months

Tools used: HTML, CSS, Bootstrap, JavaScript

Objective

The objective of the web-app designed and developed for the restaurant “ The Majestic Table” is to provide a seamless and easy dining experience. The web-app developed aims to enhance the experience by offering detailed menu and high-quality visuals. The web-app provides convenient booking capabilities, ensuring that users can make easy reservations at ‘The Majestic Table’. Additionally, the web-app allows smooth payment process integrating various payment options and hassle free transactions. The web-app developed for ‘The Majestic Table’ seeks to elevate the overall dining experience.

Problem Statement

Users need a restaurant app that would provide efficient and smooth payment as well as reservation system. Additionally, users are in need of good quality visuals and detailed descriptions about the restaurant “The Majestic Table”. They should be able to easily contact the restaurant and provide feedback through the restaurant app. An app that addresses these needs will improve user satisfaction and improve interaction.

Project Goals

  • Allow users to access comprehensive menu descriptions
  • Display vibrant and high quality images of the restaurant as well as dishes at 'The Majestic Table'
  • Develop a user-friendly reservation system
  • Enhance payment process efficiency
  • Offer simple and quick ways for users to contact the restaurant
  • Improve overall customer satisfaction with no outdated processes

Research

In this project, a goal directed approach has been taken into consideration to align the design of web-app with user needs and expectations. Firstly, in the process of user-research multiple users were interviewed about their experiences with other restaurant apps that they have used through which the challenges they faced while using the app as well as the features they liked and needed was understood. Moreover, explored whether the users are comfortable with using digital products particularly for tasks like reservations, payments and browsing menus. By identifying and understanding the challenges and frustrations of the users was able to design solutions that would address their problems. Next step taken was creating hypothetical personas which were created based on the gathered data, representing various user types and guiding design process. This method ensured that the final product was both functional as well as user-centered, meeting the expectations of the audience.

  • 1. Are you comfortable using digital products for reservations, payments and menu browsing?
  • 2. What features would you like to see added to restaurant apps?
  • 3. How would you rate your overall experience with restaurant web-apps used up till now?
  • 4. What challenges have you faced while using restaurant web-apps?
  • 5. How important is it to you to have detailed menu descriptions as well as high quality images. Additionally, a seamless payment process?
  • 6. What improvements would you suggest based on your experience?
  • 7. What features would you like to see added?

Personas

Priyanka is a 34 year old marketing manager from Pune, India. She frequently dines at fancy restaurants. She is a busy professional who desires efficiency and comfort in her dining experiences. She relies of restaurant apps which would allow her to easily browse menus, make reservations and conduct secure payment transactions. She looks for a seamless reservation system and digital payment options, avoiding outdated processes and complicated cash transactions.

Radha Deol is a 26 year old food blogger from Bangalore, India. She has a large social media following. She aims to discover and review unique restaurants, shares detailed content and high-quality images with her audience. Radha seeks restaurant apps that provide detailed and clear menu descriptions, attractive visuals and easy contact options. She avoids app which tend have poor navigation or outdated information. She aims to grow her blog by delivering engaging, reliable content.

Accessibility

Goal is to make sure that the web-app created for the fancy restaurant “The Majestic Table” is accessible to all the users

  • Made sure there is sufficient color contrast between the text and the background for readability.
  • Included hierarchical headings which ensure the order of elements on the page and elements are classified according to their level of importance
  • Ensured that all versions of the web-app are accessible If user zooms in on a particular element the rest of the page, the rest of the page automatically resizes and rearranges to fit new screen size.
  • Clear labels provided.

Accessibility was a crucial aspect of the design process to ensure that every user has an excellent experience interacting with the app.
